3. Using Ability Scores

Each character has five core ability scores that define their essential traits. These are:

  • Might: Represents the hero’s physical strength.
  • Agility: Reflects the hero’s dexterity and speed.
  • Endurance: Measures the hero’s stamina and resilience.
  • Wits: Indicates the hero’s mental acuity and focus.
  • Charm: Expresses the hero’s charisma and force of personality.

These abilities play a crucial role during gameplay, as they influence the outcome of the most important dice rolls: ability checks, saving throws, and attack rolls.

Training Rank

Heroes are skilled in various abilities, each of which progresses through five distinct levels of proficiency. All skills start at the Novice level.

  • Novice: The hero has minimal experience with the skill. They can perform basic tasks but lack the expertise to handle complex situations. Add only your ability modifier to the roll.
  • Apprentice: Some practical experience. Add ability modifier + 5.
  • Competent: Solid understanding. Add ability modifier + 10.
  • Masterful: Expert. Add ability modifier + 15.
  • Legendary: Pinnacle mastery. Add ability modifier + 20.

Your ability scores determine modifiers as follows:

4-5 -3

8-9 -1

14-15 +2

When a roll instructs you to add your ability modifier, it refers to this value.

Advantage/Disadvantage

Roll two dice instead of one:

  • Advantage: Use the higher roll.
  • Disadvantage: Use the lower roll.

Example: Roll 4 and 13 → with advantage use 13, with disadvantage use 4.

Multiple sources do not stack. If you gain both advantage and disadvantage, they cancel out.

Ability Checks

When a hero attempts a non-trivial action, they perform an ability check. The Architect decides the ability/skill involved and sets the DC.

DC Difficulty Description
5 Trivial task — success is almost guaranteed
10 Easy task — minimal effort
15 Moderate task — standard challenge
20 Hard task — requires skill/focus
25 Very hard task — only experts succeed
30 Nearly impossible — only exceptional success

Roll a 20-sided die and add the relevant ability modifier. Success = result ≥ DC.

Skills

Characters possess abilities, not skills by default. Skills represent specialized training and appear on the sheet only if trained.

  • If not trained: use only ability modifier.
  • If trained: follow normal training rank rules (Novice → Legendary).

Ability–Skill Associations

Each trained skill is linked to an ability:

  • Might: Athletics
  • Agility: Acrobatics, Dexterity, Piloting (Flying Vehicles), Piloting (Land Vehicles), Piloting (Aquatic Vehicles), Stealth
  • Endurance: *(No default skills)*
  • Wits: Animal Handling, Insight, Investigation, Lore (History, Medicine, Nature, Religion, Technology), Perception, Survival
  • Charm: Deception, Intimidation, Performance, Persuasion

These are suggestions. The Architect may add additional skills. Final authority rests with the Architect.

Helping

Two characters (PCs or NPCs) can assist each other with skill checks, adding the helper's total modifier (ability + training + other modifiers) to the acting character's roll.

Assistance is possible if:

  • The helper could reasonably attempt the task themselves.
  • The helper’s actions meaningfully contribute.

Examples:

  • Recalling information about a location: helper can add Lore (History).
  • Driving a helicopter: another character cannot use their own motorcycle Piloting modifier, but may assist using Lore (Technology) to explain systems.

The GM has final authority over assistance and applicable modifiers.
